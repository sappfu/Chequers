Chequers will be used by the University of Texas at Arlington’s College of Engineering for recruiting and outreach purposes.The purpose of the project is to showcase the checkers-playing robot to inspire potential future students and provide a source of public relations for the University of Texas at Arlington. As the Chequers system will be used for demonstration purposes for the university, it should be interesting enough to encourage people to continue playing.
The robot system, which has been named Chequers, will use a vision subsystem to determine the state of the board and an Artificial Intelligent (AI) subsystem to determine which moves the robot will make during the game. When making a move, the robot arm will be able to move a piece to the correct square, jump over the human player’s pieces, remove captured pieces from the board, and crown the human player’s piece, as per the rules of the game. The human player will be able to manually move their pieces, after which, the vision subsystem will check the state of the board, verifying that the move was legal, and allow the AI to determine the next move. The whole system should not be more than 2’l x 2’w x 1.5’h, and will have a budget of $800. As the Chequers system will be used for demonstration purposes for the university, it will be required to have a polished look. The user interface may include voice recognition and speech. Chequers will have multiple levels of AI difficulty to make it fun for players of different skill levels. All features of the project are defined in the System Requirements Specification. The system will need to be reasonably easy to transport by car or van, and have a short setup time. Due to the nature of the project, the system will need to be robust enough to withstand multiple breakdowns and setups.
